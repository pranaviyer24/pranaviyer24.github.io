Case study: NGO website re-design

Proud Indian are a non-profit organization who focus on low-income communities, creating small scale business models to create a self-sustaining society. I was tasked with redesigning their website by re-organizing it and give it a more modern feel.

Objectives

I determined the objectives of the website as the following:

  1. To ensure the users learn about the goals/activities of the NGO so they can decide whether they want to contribute
  2. To create a simple process to contribute to the NGO by volunteering or donating.

Process

Analysis of existing website

The Home page does not immediately show any important information. The entire banner is occupied by quotes and do not give any information about the NGO. The mission statement and vision are seen only after scrolling down.

The information is improperly organized — the About Us page consisted of the founding story but nothing else. Aside from the mission statement on the homepage there is not much indication of what the NGO does unless the user clicks on ‘Initiatives’. More history can be added

The information at the top of the page of the ‘Get involved’ is not as important as the different volunteering plans, the flow of information needs to be improved.

User interviews

A total of 10 users were interviewed and were asked to use the donation page. All interviewees agreed that the options provided were confusing and when asked if they would prefer to donate a custom amount — the answer was always yes. Furthermore it was observed that reading every single option took a significant amount of time. I determined that the donation page needed to feature the custom amount more prominently
